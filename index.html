Q Learning for Optimal Trade Execution
IEOR 222 - Financial Engineering II (Final Project)
Paper  /  Code  

Optimal Order Execution has been a long standing problem in financial institutions. There are many ways to tackle this issue; however, the backbone of all the solutions was a strategy laid out by Dimitris Berstsimas and Andrew Lo in the paper Optimal control of execution costs. The goal of the final project was to use reinforcement learning, specifically Q Learning, in order to learn the optimal order execution strategy under various price impact models that was laid out in Bertimas and Lo's paper.
